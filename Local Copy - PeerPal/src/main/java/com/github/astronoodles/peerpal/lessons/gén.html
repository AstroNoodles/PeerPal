Gender Lesson 1

Part 1

Error with gender agreement between a noun and its article and/or adjective. Adjectives must agree in gender! If the noun you are describing is feminine you must make the adjective agree by changing the “o” to an “a”. If the noun you are describing is masculine make the adjective agree by leaving the “o”.


Note: There are some exceptions:

  • Nouns that end in “ión”, “dad”, and “tud” are feminine (la ciudad, la juventud, la ciudad)
  • Nouns that end in “ma” are masculine (el clima)
Other irregulars include:
  • El Agua
  • El Mapa


Part 2

In Spanish, nouns are only classified as masculine or feminine.


Masculine Nouns:

  • refer to male beings (el padre - father, el señor - Mr.)
  • end in -o (el curso - course, el cuaderno - notebook)
  • La foto (picture) and la mano (hand) do not follow this rule as they are feminine nouns.
  • numbers (el dos - two)
  • days of the week (el día - day, el lunes - Monday)
  • months of the year (el abril - April)
  • Names of rivers, oceans, seas, and lakes (el Amazonas - the Amazon, el Pacífico - pacific)

Feminine Nouns:

  • Refer to female beings .(la madre - mother, la señora - Mrs.)
  • End in -a (la escuela- school, la nota - grade)
  • día does not follow this rule as it is a masculine noun.
  • El agua also does not follow the rule
  • End in -ción (la estación - station)
  • End in -dad (la ciudad - city)
  • End in -tad (la libertad - liberty)
  • End in -ie (la serie - series)
  • End in -umbre (la costumbre - custom)
  • Letters of the alphabet (la a)

Nouns that end in -ista can be masculine or feminine depending on to whom they refer.

  • El artista - (male) artist
  • El futbolista - (male) soccer player
  • La artista - (female) artist
  • La futbolista - (female) soccer player


Part 3

There are a group of nouns of Greek origin that end in -ma, -ta, -pa which are masculine although they seem to be feminine because they end in -a.

  • El problema - problem
  • El planeta - planet
  • El mapa - map

The best way to know what gender the nouns are is to look at or learn the definite articles (the) that are with the noun. El is the masculine definite article and la is the feminine definite article.
